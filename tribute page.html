ABOUT SACHIN TENDULKAR :

Sachin Tendulkar is a former Indian cricketer and one of the greatest batsmen in the history of the sport. He was born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, India, and began playing cricket at a very young age. His incredible talent was evident from the start, and he made his debut for the Indian cricket team at the age of just 16. Over the course of his career, Tendulkar scored more than 34,000 runs and recorded 100 international centuries, making him the highest run scorer in both Test and ODI cricket. He was also the first player to score a double century in ODI cricket, and he holds a number of other records and achievements in the sport. Tendulkar was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 2013. What set Tendulkar apart from other players was not just his impressive statistics, but his sheer skill and mastery of the game. He was known for his incredible hand-eye coordination, his quick reflexes, and his ability to play a wide range of shots with precision and power. He was equally comfortable playing spin and pace bowling, and he could adapt to any situation in a match with ease. Off the field, Tendulkar was widely respected and admired for his humble and gracious demeanor. He was a role model for young cricketers and fans all around the world, and he inspired countless people with his dedication, hard work, and passion for the sport. Tendulkar retired from cricket in 2013, but his legacy lives on. He remains one of the most beloved and respected figures in Indian cricket and a true icon of the sport.

RECORDES AND AHIVEVENMETS :

  • Most runs scored in Test cricket (15,921)
  • Most runs scored in ODI cricket (18,426)
  • First player to score a double century in ODI cricket
  • Most centuries in international cricket (100)
  • Most man of the match awards in international cricket (62)
  • One of the Wisden Cricketers of the Year in 1997
  • ICC Cricketer of the Year in 2010
